02 Supply chain & logistics — end to end

The operating model most small brands run on Full-package (FPP/FOB) manufacturing overseas, on purchase orders with 30% deposit / 70% before ship, selling DTC on Shopify, with shipping outsourced to a 3PL. That’s the spine; everything below hangs off it.

Product & spec

The chain starts with a tech pack (measurements, grading, bill of materials, trims, labels), not the factory. Tools: Techpacker / Backbone PLM. Look for OEKO-TEX / Lenzing TENCEL certs.

Sourcing materials

Fabric minimums are brutal (500–1,000+ yd/kg per color) — an FPP factory absorbs this for you. Don’t forget trims: elastic, drawcords, thread, woven label, care label, hangtags, polybags.

Manufacturing

Best modal-knit regions: Portugal (premium, low MOQ), Peru (pima), India (organic/value), Pakistan (loungewear), China (flexible), Turkey, USA-LA (small batch). Find via Maker’s Row, Sewport, a sourcing agent (~5–10%), or trade shows (MAGIC, Texworld, Curve).

Sampling & QC

Gates: proto → fit → PP (pre-production) → size set → TOP. Approve the PP sample in writing before bulk. Use AQL 2.5 inspection (QIMA / SGS / Bureau Veritas). Don’t pay the 70% balance until inspection passes.

Freight & Incoterms

FOB is the small-brand default; DDP (delivered to your door, duties paid) is the easiest for your first 1–2 orders. Ocean (4–6 wks, LCL for small volume) vs air (days, 5–10× cost). Plan ~90–150 days from PO to warehouse on run one.

Customs & compliance

Apparel duties are high (~8–32%) — man-made fiber > cotton — plus Section 301 on China. Need a customs bond, ISF “10+2” filing, commercial invoice, packing list. DDP bundles this while you learn.

Fulfillment

Self-fulfill the first small drop to learn it, then move to a 3PL (ShipBob, ShipMonk, Red Stag, FBA). Branded unboxing is cheap differentiation — put the family story on the card.

Sleepwear regulatory note Adult sleepwear still needs FTC fiber-content %, a care label (16 CFR 423), country of origin, and your company name or a free RN number. Children’s sleepwear adds strict flammability rules (CPSC 16 CFR 1615/1616) — the reason v1 is adults-only.

03 Contracts & legal checklist

  • NDA before sharing designs with any factory.
  • Trademark “Quitters” in Class 25 (clothing) — do a knockout search and file early. Your most important asset.
  • Manufacturing / Supply Agreement — price, MOQ, lead times, terms, QC standard, defect remedy, and design IP ownership.
  • Purchase Order per production run (styles, qty, price, ship date, Incoterm).
  • Supplier Code of Conduct (labor/ethics — matters for retail buyers later).
  • Freight & 3PL service agreements with SLAs and damage/loss liability.
  • Entity + sales tax + product-liability insurance (essential for anything worn).

04 Costing & cash reality

Landed cost = FOB price + freight/unit + duty + customs/broker/insurance + 3PL inbound. Target roughly 4–5× landed → retail to survive discounts, returns, and marketing.

Cash is the constraint, not demand. You pay the deposit and the balance ~90 days before selling a single set. Manage it with small runs, pre-orders/drops, and a tight colorway count.

07 Where Quitters wins white space

  1. Fit is the category’s soft underbelly. Reviews everywhere complain of runs-large / inconsistent sizing and short sleeves for tall women. “Pajamas that actually fit” + true-to-size + a tall option is a defensible wedge.
  2. Quality details — light colors going see-through is a repeat gripe. Get opacity, fabric weight, and a real pocket right and earn review love cheaply.
  3. Brand emotion is wide open. The lane splits into sterile minimalism (Eberjey/Lunya) and loud prints (Printfresh). Nobody owns warm, funny, family-cozy — which is exactly “you got your quitters on.”
  4. Approachable-premium price gap (~$78–98) sits just under the pile-up — strong for gifting (holiday, Mother’s Day, bridal, new-mom).
Recommended positioning “Quitters — the softest permission slip you’ll ever own.” Premium-feeling TENCEL modal sets, fit you don’t fight, a brand that’s warm and funny instead of precious. Lead product: the modal set (long + short), 2–3 colorways, true-to-size + tall. Entry ~$78–98 (verify vs landed cost). First traction lever: gifting + the authentic family origin story.

08 Target buyer (to validate)

Women 28–55, comfort- and self-care-oriented, shops DTC/Instagram, buys for herself and gifts. Emotional driver: rest as a small rebellion — “I’ve earned the right to quit for the night.” Secondary motion: gifting, then eventually his-and-hers / family sets (Lake proves it works).

09 Homework before spending real money

  • Buy the top 3 (Eberjey, Cozy Earth, MeUndies modal, ~$350). Wear-test softness, fit, opacity, washing — set the bar you must clear. Highest-ROI spend you’ll make.
  • Mine reviews — read 100+ 1–3☆ competitor reviews and tally complaint themes. Free and brutal.
  • Name/voice test — does “Quitters” read cozy-funny or negative to cold audiences? Run a cheap IG split (family is lovingly biased).
  • Trademark knockout search in Class 25 — now, before falling in love.
  • Price & gifting survey — what target women last paid and how often they gift sleepwear.
  • Color & weight — validate 2–3 launch colors and modal gsm against opacity / too-thin / too-hot complaints.
